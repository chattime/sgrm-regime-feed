1 · What this system does

It produces one decision every morning: "Sell N TQQQ puts at strike $X, in 3 batches at triggers A / B / C."

Everything in the platform serves that single output. The decision is:

  • Backed by the highest historical win-rate for the current market state.
  • Aware of price action (close, 10/40-day highs & lows, RSI, VXN, streak).
  • Aware of official news (FOMC, CPI, NFP, earnings) and ad-hoc shocks.
  • Aware of your account margin — it won't size you into a forced liquidation.
  • Expressed as direction + sizing + ladder, ready to pre-key as GTC orders.
The core trade: you sell cash-secured / margin short puts on TQQQ (a 3× leveraged Nasdaq-100 ETF) about 12–16% out-of-the-money, ~45 days out. You collect premium up front and keep it all if TQQQ stays above your strike. The system's job is to tell you how much to sell and when not to.

2 · Your daily routine

  1. The VM runs the engine automatically at 09:00 ET (pre-open) and posts the plan to Telegram + your sheet.
  2. Read the Telegram message — it tells you in plain English: trade today or stand down, and the 3 batch triggers.
  3. Open the dashboard → Plan tab to see the ladder, then Analysis to see why.
  4. If it's a GO: pre-key 3 GTC limit orders tonight at the batch trigger prices. Whatever fills tomorrow, fills.
  5. Check Positions for anything hitting a profit target or needing defense.
  6. A second verify run at 10:05 ET re-checks with post-open data and only re-alerts if the plan changed.
If it says GREEN day / NO ENTRY: do nothing new. Manage existing positions only. Patience is part of the edge — most of the system's value is in the days it tells you not to trade.

Strike selection (anchor + δ cap)

Old rigid "X% OTM" logic breaks in high volatility — at 80% IV a 10%-OTM put has δ ≈ −0.30, not −0.20. So the picker uses a price anchor for targeting and a delta cap for safety:

anchor = spot × (1 − 0.025 intraday_drop − 0.10 margin_of_safety)   ≈ spot × 0.875

Then: compute IV + δ locally (Black-Scholes) on every liquid strike, keep only those with |δ| ≤ cap, and pick the one closest to the anchor. If no strike passes the cap → NO ENTRY (the vol regime is too hot to sell safely).

Worked example — spot $77.52, IV 80%, 39 DTE

Unit weighting per batch shifts with regime: RALLY front-loads (50/30/20), DOWNTREND back-loads (20/30/50) to buy deeper discounts in a falling tape. B1 rule: take the better of the table trigger vs. the intraday low.

Exits (Section C — 3 GTC profit rungs)

Queue all three exit rungs at once. Buyback price = credit × (1 − target%).

SIDEWAYS+25%, close 50%+45%, close 30%+75%, close 20%

Event tightening: near a Tier-A event, pull Rung 1 in so winners clear pre-catalyst (0–1d +12% · 2d +15% · 3d +20%).

Defense (Rules A · B · E)

  • Roll only if profit-remaining < 15% and DTE ≤ 10, for ≥ $0.10/ct net credit, same/lower strike, 45–60 DTE — max one roll.
  • RALLY/SIDEWAYS: roll for credit OK. DOWNTREND: take assignment, do not roll (credit-rolling caused 115 forced cuts in the 2022 bear backtest).
  • Close highest-δ positions first to cut assignment risk.

Intraday scalp (Rule B) — short-put harvest

Separate from the daily plan. When a sudden one-off shock spikes intraday fear, IV jumps and puts get richly priced — so you sell puts to harvest the inflated premium, then buy them back as the panic fades. It's a short-put play (not buying puts), and it does not count toward the daily unit cap.

4 · The guards — why it sometimes says NO ENTRY

Before any sizing, the plan runs layered guards. Any one failing → NO ENTRY. Layer 1 holds the two highest-priority circuit breakers.

Streak Drawdown Brake Layer 1

Watches how far TQQQ has fallen from where the current red streak began (the anchor = first close of the streak).

BandConditionAction
GREENdrawdown ≥ −15%normal
AMBER−22% … −15%finish only if nearest strike has >12% OTM buffer (soft)
REDdrawdown < −22%units = 0
HARD-STOPany open strike within 8% of spotunits = 0 (checked first, all bands)

Backtest (2010–2026): v7.5 + this brake = −$4,361 P/L (vs −$11,576) and max drawdown $7,124 (vs $17,414). The HARD-STOP is the live lever.

Excess-Liquidity guard Layer 1 · v3.6

The brake watches price; this watches your account. Excess Liquidity is the broker's cushion before it force-liquidates your positions:

Excess Liquidity = Equity-with-Loan-Value − Maintenance-Margin-Requirement

When it hits $0 the broker starts auto-selling. As TQQQ falls, the margin requirement on your short puts rises and net-liq drops, so this cushion shrinks faster than price. The guard halts new entries while the cushion is still healthy.

BandCushion (EL ÷ net-liq)Action
GREEN≥ 20%normal
AMBER10–20%half-size new entries (×0.5)
RED< 10% or EL < $15,000NO ENTRY
This is the real force-selling trigger — not buying power. Buying power answers "can I open a new trade?"; excess liquidity answers "will the broker close my existing trades?" The second is what wrecks accounts.

Live Tiger data only. With no live account data the guard is inactive (it passes — never blocks on estimates), and the Telegram alert will say so explicitly so a silent fallback never goes unnoticed.

Other gates & the news overlay

Systemic shut-offs & core gates

  • 10-day-high brake — close < −15% from the 10-day high → stop new batches.
  • Streak halt — red streak ≥ 6 and VXN > 40.
  • VXN — 35–45 = half size + wider strike; > 45 = halt.
  • RSI — > 75 (70 in RALLY) = overbought block; < 35 + streak ≥ 2 = capitulation block.
  • Macro — Tier-A event within 3 days tightens behaviour.

Layer-4 empirical filters

  • 5-day return floor — block if 5d return < −7%.
  • vs-10d-high floor — block if > 10% below the 10-day high. (Backtested 2015–26: this hard −10% block beats softer variants; it earns its keep catching deep-drop losers in 2022/2025.)
  • RSI oversold — block if RSI < 35 with a 2+ day red streak.

Layer-5 news modes (3-mode classifier)

ModeMeaningSizeδ cap
CLEARnormal tape×1.00.24
CAUTIONone-sided / likely noise fear spike×0.5 (harvest rich premium at half size)0.27
SHOCKstructural panic×0 (NO ENTRY)0.20

The key idea: a fear spike isn't always danger — sometimes it's the best premium. CAUTION lets you harvest it at half size rather than blocking outright; SHOCK stands fully aside.

Event calendars (structural + macro)

Two calendars ride with every plan — they're informational context, not gates, and show in the Analysis calendar card plus a macro summary point under the Plan.

Structural (rule-computed)

Monthly OPEX, Quad-witching, NFP, month-end & quarter-end rebalance, Russell reconstitution, and VIX expiry — each with date + days-until, flagged when ≤ 2 days out. The dashboard shows the full table.

Macro (hand-maintained econ-calendar)

FOMC, CPI, PPI, PCE, Jackson Hole — date-varying, so they live in a dated list (ECON_CALENDAR) in the engine that needs a yearly refresh from the Fed/BLS/BEA schedules. Events within 5 days surface as the summary point under the plan, e.g. "FOMC Day 1 today — decision tomorrow." These also appear in the regime report; the engine calendar is the always-present machine-readable source.

6 · Buying power vs. excess liquidity (plain English)

Buying Power = "How much new can I open?" It sets the unit count: units ≈ floor(BP × 40% ÷ (strike × 100)). Because the strike tracks TQQQ's price, your unit count moves with both your BP and the TQQQ price.
Excess Liquidity = "Will the broker force-sell me?" It's the cushion above the maintenance-margin requirement. Hit $0 and the broker liquidates your positions automatically — usually at the worst possible moment. This is the metric that actually causes blow-ups, which is why it's a hard Layer-1 guard.

For you: both come from Tiger automatically via the morning engine run — you don't type them in (leave the Settings EL blank). For a friend: they have no Tiger access, so they type their own BP and EL into Settings, and the dashboard re-sizes / re-warns on their numbers.

7 · Telegram alerts

All alerts come from @SGRM_WSP_bot into the shared WSP group. Message types:

  • 🟢 Trade today — sell N $X puts; lists the 3 batch triggers and total credit.
  • 🟢 Green day — exit mode — TQQQ closed up; take profits, no new sells.
  • 🟡 Don't trade today — a gate blocked entry; lists the plain-English reason.
  • 🚨 Stand down — overnight news shock; manage existing only.

Extra lines you may see: 📅 calendar (witching / quarter-end), ⚡ intraday scalp levels, and the margin line:

  • 🛡 Margin cushion OK / 🟠 Margin watch / 🛑 MARGIN ALERT — your excess-liquidity band.
  • ⚠ Tiger account data missing — live data didn't arrive; EL guard inactive today, check margin manually.
  • ⏳ Market data not ready — price bar isn't in yet; no plan generated, will retry.
Important for the group: the strike / triggers / regime are universal, but the units, BP committed, and EL in the message are computed from the operator's account. Everyone re-sizes to their own capital (Sizing A/B) and judges margin from their own EL.
